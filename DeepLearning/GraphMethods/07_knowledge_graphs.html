Beyond Interaction Graphs — Adding Semantics

Interaction graph only knows:

  • "User A bought Item X"
  • No WHY, no properties, no relationships

Knowledge Graph adds:

  • Attributes: brand, category, price tier
  • Relations: belongs_to, compatible_with
  • Hierarchies: category → subcategory → item
An interaction graph knows iPhone was purchased. A KG knows iPhone is a smartphone, made by Apple, in the premium segment, compatible with Lightning cables.

Knowledge Graph Structure

Entities: items, categories, brands, attributes (nodes)

Relations: typed edges — belongs_to, has_brand, has_price_tier, compatible_with

Triples: \((h, r, t)\) — e.g., (iPhone, has_brand, Apple)


Formal definition:

\[ \mathcal{KG} = \{(h, r, t) \;|\; h, t \in \mathcal{E},\; r \in \mathcal{R}\} \]

A KG turns your catalog into a richly connected semantic network. Every product is described not just by its ID, but by its place in the world of products.

KG Embeddings — TransE, RotatE, ComplEx

Goal: embed entities and relations in vector space such that \(\mathbf{h} + \mathbf{r} \approx \mathbf{t}\)

TransE (Bordes et al., 2013):

\[ f(h,r,t) = -\|\mathbf{h} + \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{t}\|_{L_1/L_2} \]

Intuition: relation \(\mathbf{r}\) translates head to tail in embedding space

Limitation: cannot model 1-to-N relations (multiple tails for same head+relation)


RotatE (Sun et al., 2019):

\[ f(h,r,t) = -\|\mathbf{h} \circ \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{t}\| \quad \text{where } \mathbf{r} \in \mathbb{C},\; |r_i| = 1 \]

Rotation in complex plane — models symmetry, antisymmetry, inversion, composition


ComplEx (Trouillon et al., 2016):

\[ f(h,r,t) = \text{Re}\!\left(\langle \mathbf{h}, \mathbf{r}, \bar{\mathbf{t}} \rangle\right) = \text{Re}\!\left(\sum_k h_k \cdot r_k \cdot \bar{t}_k\right) \]

Hermitian dot product in complex space — handles antisymmetric relations

Training: contrastive learning — score true triples higher than corrupted ones (negative sampling)
